Professor Messer's Exam Hacks Cheat Sheet

Strategic guidelines and operational parameters for maximize your exam score

Rule #4

Board Allocation Maximization Mapping

CompTIA scores questions based on weighting systems where operations (Domain 4) and vulnerability mitigation (Domain 2) hold the largest portions (50% combined). Align your review cycles to secure these high-yield domains first.

Weight Allocations Matrix:
Domain Exam Weight Blueprint Focus Areas
General Security Concepts 12% Controls classifications, Zero Trust plane division, PKI mechanics.
Threats, Vulns & Mitigations 22% TOCTOU, memory injections, social engineering vectors, DDoS amplifications.
Security Architecture 18% Cloud models, VM escape protection, WAF vs NGFW, data protection.

Rule #10

Flag & Move Sequencing Parameters

Time management is the single highest failure point on the Security+ exam. Keep your momentum going and avoid analysis paralysis.

Execution Checklist:
  • If a question takes more than 45 seconds to parse, make a tentative choice immediately.
  • Press the Flag button to mark the question.
  • Move to the next question immediately. Do not sit and over-analyze.
  • Only return to review flagged questions during the "final loop" after completing all other items.
Rule #11

Immediate MCQ-First Routing (Defer PBQs)

Performance Based Questions (PBQs) appear at the very beginning of the test. They require multi-step drag-and-drop operations or terminal emulation configurations and consume massive amounts of time.

Execution Blueprint:
  • When the exam starts, do NOT attempt to solve the first 3 to 7 questions if they are PBQs.
  • Flag them immediately, select no answers, and skip past them to the first Multiple Choice Question (MCQ).
  • Complete all 75+ multiple-choice questions first. This secures your foundation score.
  • Solve the skipped PBQs during your final loop with the remaining time, knowing exactly how many minutes you have left.
Rule #13

Zero-Penalty Complete Selection Mandate

CompTIA does not deduct points for incorrect selections. A blank question is guaranteed to be 0 points, whereas an educated guess holds a 25%+ chance of adding points.

Mandate Policies:
  • Never submit the exam with any blank items.
  • Our Practice Engine blocks submission and forces guess inputs if questions are left empty before clicking final submit.
  • Use process of elimination to cross out two clearly incorrect options, then choose from the remaining two.

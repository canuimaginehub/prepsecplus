## Page 5

Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): A document outlining rules and guidelines for

proper and acceptable use of an organization's computer systems and

resources.

Business Continuity: The planning and preparations to ensure an

- rganization can maintain essential operations during and after disruptions.
Disaster Recovery: The process of restoring an organization's IT systems

and data after a major disruption or disaster.

Incident Response: A structured approach to managing and mitigating

cybersecurity incidents.

Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC): The systematic process for

designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software.

Playbooks: Predefined sets of procedures for responding to specific

security incidents or events.

Owners: Individuals or entities responsible for overall accountability for

systems and data.

Controllers: Those determining how and why data is processed.

Processors: Entities processing data on behalf of data controllers.

Custodians/Stewards: Individuals safeguarding and managing data.

Risk Identification: Identifying potential risks and threats to an

- rganization.
Ad Hoc Risk Assessment: Sporadic risk assessments conducted as needed.

Recurring Risk Assessment: Regularly scheduled risk assessments.

One-time Risk Assessment: A single assessment conducted for a specific

purpose.

Continuous Risk Assessment: Ongoing monitoring and assessment of risks.

Qualitative Risk Analysis: Evaluating risks based on subjective criteria.

Quantitative Risk Analysis: Assessing risks using numerical data and

probabilities.

Single Loss Expectancy (SLE): Expected monetary loss from a single security

incident.

Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE): Expected annual loss from a specific

risk.

Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO): Estimated frequency of a specific

risk occurring.

Probability: The chance of a risk event occurring.

Likelihood: The probability of a risk event happening.

## Page 6

Exposure Factor: Percentage of loss if a risk occurs.

Impact: Extent of consequences if a risk materializes.

Business Impact Analysis: Assessment of potential impacts of risks on

business operations.

Recovery Time Objective (RTO): Maximum allowable downtime for system

recovery.

Recovery Point Objective (RPO): Acceptable data loss during recovery.

Mean Time to Repair (MTTR): Average time to restore a system or service.

Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF): Average time between system

failures.

Penetration Testing: Simulating attacks to identify vulnerabilities.

Right-to-Audit Clause: Contractual provision allowing audits of a vendor's

security practices.

Service-Level Agreement (SLA): Formal contract specifying service

expectations.

Memorandum of Agreement (MOA): Less formal agreement outlining

mutual intentions.

Memorandum of Understanding (MOU): Non-binding agreement outlining

common understanding.

Master Service Agreement (MSA): Comprehensive contract setting terms

and conditions.

Work Order (WO)/Statement of Work (SOW): Documents specifying

project or service requirements.

Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA): Contract protecting confidential

information.

Business Partners Agreement (BPA): Agreement between business partners

- utlining terms and responsibilities.
Data Subject: Individual to whom data relates.

Controller vs. Processor: Roles in managing and processing data.

Ownership: Legal rights to data or assets.

Data Inventory and Retention: Cataloging data and defining retention

periods.

Right to Be Forgotten: The right of individuals to have their data erased.

Attestation: Confirming compliance through statements or evidence.
